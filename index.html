The work I've been part of at UMW with Domain of One's Own encouraging faculty and students to get their own domain and web hosting has a pretty long history that pre-dates my time at Mary Washington. More than ten years ago now, my friend Zach Davis turned me on to the world of commodity web hosting. He spun up a server and encouraged me---along with a bunch of his friends around the country---to get a domain. What's more, he helped us point those domains to his server and gave a bunch of us cPanel accounts so we could actually use it. He created a hosting co-operative, similar to what Tim Owens did with Hippie Hosting two years ago, and what's now doing together with Reclaim Hosting. I had no idea how important that offer of a domain and web hosting would be to my career more than a decade later.

Web hosting has been the platform that has helped me to understand how the web works. So much of the work I'm part of now is premised on the idea that the best way to learn about the web is to actually build your own little piece of it. I approach this from my own experience. The technical stuff has never been intuitive for me, I've spent a lot of time trying to grok how domains, servers, code, databases, etc. work together to make the web. I'm still struggling with that, and the work with WordPress and other open source applications on a LAMP server stack has proven invaluable for wrapping my head around this stuff.

So, why the nostalgic look back in time? Because ten years later I find myself part of a broader effort to help educate people about the web. And that starts with reclaiming your piece of the web. I've benefitted immensely from what I learned about web hosting and domains, and I see the role of Reclaim Your Domain as similar to what Zach did for many of us back in the early oughts. Provide as many people as possible with a hands-on platform for understanding of how the web works.

How? Well, by using this space to share my own process of taking stock of my work on the web in an attempt to organize, archive, and explain all the while. I'll be using the blog here, as well as my personal blog bavatuesdays to narrate my own process of reclaiming my domain.
